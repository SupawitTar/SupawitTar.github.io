Biography

Dr. Supawit Tangpanithandee is a researcher and clinician with a unique dual competency in medicine and computational data science. He earned his Doctor of Medicine (M.D.) from the Faculty of Medicine Ramathibodi Hospital, Mahidol University, and a Master of Science (M.Sc.) in Computer Science at Thammasat University. His master's thesis focused on predictive analysis in education, and he holds a Diploma in Clinical Epidemiology from Chiang Mai University.

Currently, Dr. Tangpanithandee conducts advanced research at the Mayo Clinic, investigating AI in Nephrology, pharmacoinformatics, and clinical outcomes research. His work applies ML and LLMs to predict and prevent complications such as cardiac surgery-associated AKI, analyze drug–drug interactions, and study long-term outcomes of kidney transplant recipients.

Research Interests

I bridge clinical nephrology and computational methods. Below are current focus areas and representative workstreams.

  • Artificial Intelligence in Medicine

    Using LLMs for literature synthesis and drug interaction checking; automated ML for AKI and transplant risk prediction; consensus clustering for patient subtyping.

  • Clinical Nephrology

    Research on kidney transplantation outcomes, ICU-related AKI, and electrolyte/acid–base disorders with direct clinical translation.

  • Pharmacoinformatics & Clinical Pharmacology

    Analysis of drug–drug interactions across databases and AI tools, polypharmacy risk assessment, and development of decision-support strategies for complex patients.
